# Backlog Refinement

Last Updated: 2026-03-29
Audience: Product Owner and Claude Code
Purpose: On-demand process for preparing and prioritizing future sprint work

---

## When to Trigger

- Product Owner explicitly requests it
- Sprint just completed and priorities may have shifted
- New feature idea surfaces mid-sprint
- A backlog item is more than 3 sprints old and may be stale
- A completed sprint unlocks or blocks other planned work

---

## Process (30-60 minutes)

### Step 1: Prepare (5 min)
- Read current `docs/ALL_SPRINTS_MASTER_PLAN.md`
- Note the last completed sprint and current state

### Step 2: Backlog Review (10 min)
- Scan all Next Sprint Candidates
- Identify any items that are stale, blocked, or no longer relevant
- Flag items with dependencies on other items

### Step 3: Prioritization Discussion (15 min)
- Discuss with Product Owner: value, effort, risk for each candidate
- Re-rank priorities based on current goals
- Promote HOLD items if circumstances have changed

### Step 4: Estimation Updates (5 min)
- Calibrate effort estimates based on recent sprint velocity
- If a sprint took significantly longer than estimated, adjust similar items

### Step 5: Add New Items (10 min)
- Capture any new work identified during discussion
- Minimum viable entry: title + priority
- Items more than 3 sprints out do not need full acceptance criteria yet

### Step 6: Cleanup and Documentation (5 min)
- Remove obsolete items (or move to HOLD with reason)
- Update `docs/ALL_SPRINTS_MASTER_PLAN.md`
- Note any items that have blockers or dependencies

---

## Definition of Ready

A backlog item is ready to sprint when it has:
- Clear, bounded scope
- Measurable acceptance criteria
- Effort estimate
- No unresolved blockers
- Priority relative to other items

---

## Refinement Modes

| Mode | Time | Use When |
|------|------|----------|
| Quick Priority Shuffle | 15 min | Just finished a sprint, reorder top items |
| New Feature Scoping | 30 min | New idea needs decomposition |
| Full Refinement | 60 min | Backlog is stale or major shift in priorities |
